## What AI-DOS has become

After 15 missions and three shipped products, AI-DOS is **two companies in one repository**:

1. **AI-DOS (the OS)** — A Git-native operating system for one-person AI software companies. Proven: missions, compiler, asset registry, repository intelligence, execution plans, merge-governance.

2. **Get Paid Toolkit (the portfolio)** — A freelancer payment-operations suite emerging from M003's invoice follow-up thesis, delivered as static copy-tools on Hostinger.

These are not in conflict. M007 made AI-DOS the primary product; M013–M015 proved the OS can birth and select real portfolio companies. The portfolio **validates** the OS.

**AI-DOS is naturally becoming a Freelancer Payment Operations company** — not a generic AI productivity platform, not a developer utilities shop, not a portfolio-of-random-SaaS factory.

## What we learned (executive lessons)

**Strengths:** Mission memory works; organizational learning beats fresh brainstorms (M015); static wedges ship fast; UI craft improved M013→M014; merge-governance scales.

**Mistakes / risks:** M006 Phase B never completed — WTP for standalone chaser remains unverified; three products shipped while validation paused; suite UX fragmented (README links only in P001/P002); no usage analytics; perpetual-wedge risk without suite cohesion or platform.

**Weaknesses to address:** Same ICP, three visual identities; no shared code; no data handoff between tools; D006 validation gate bypassed by wedge strategy.

---

## Final recommendation

**B. Unify the products into a single suite.**

Not another independent product. Not a market pivot. The repository spent M013–M015 converging on one suite narrative; fragmentation is the documented gap (`tasks/Backlog.md`, M015 report §7). Unification is the lowest-risk, highest-evidence next move before any fourth product or M003 SaaS platform.
